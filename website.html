        <span class="tag">Evidence #1</span>

        <h2>Media <em>&amp;</em> Persuasion</h2>

        <p class="sub">Looking at Advertisements</p>

        <div class="rule"></div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p>The lesson that made the biggest impression on me this term was about how advertisements try to convince people to buy things. We learned about three major strategies that creators use when they make posters or videos. They are called ethos, logos, and pathos.</p>

        <p>Before we studied this in class, I used to look at ads on my phone and think they were just random pictures. Now I know that everything in the picture is put there for a reason to make me feel a certain way. Pathos is when they try to make you feel emotional, like showing a sad animal so you donate your money. Logos is when they give you facts or numbers, like saying a food has certain vitamins, so you think it makes sense to buy it. Ethos is when they use a famous person or a doctor so you trust the brand more.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <div class="pullquote">

          <p>This changed how I look at my social media feed. I always try to spot the trick they are using on me now. It stops me from wasting money because I can see exactly what the company is doing to get my attention.</p>

        </div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p>It also helped me write better. I used to just write down my points with no real order. Now I pick a strategy. If I want to make a strong point, I can use facts to talk to the reader's brain, or I can tell a sad story to talk to their feelings.</p>

      </div>

        <h2>The <em>Last</em> Chance</h2>

        <p class="sub">Creative Writing Assignment</p>

        <div class="rule"></div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <div class="story-card">

          <h3>The Plot</h3>

          <p>A kid named Brad has to leave Mars because a massive rock is about to hit the planet. He gets put on a small escape pod by his parents, who stay behind because the ship is full. He crashes on Earth in a forest and finds a dog named Charlie. They live in the woods and survive together, but then Charlie gets pinned down by a fallen tree trunk. Brad has to choose between using his last power crystal to heal the dog, or saving the crystal to call space and check if his parents lived. He picks the dog.</p>

        </div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p>This piece of work shows that I used these skills:</p>

        <div class="badges">

          <div class="badge on">✦ Communication</div>

          <div class="badge on">✦ Critical Thinking</div>

          <div class="badge">Self-Directed Learning</div>

        </div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p><strong>Critical Thinking and Problem Solving:</strong> The rules say this skill is about looking at details to solve tricky problems and make smart choices. I used this when I built the plot of my story. I had to figure out a realistic way for a kid to find food and survive in a new place. I also had to create a big problem at the climax where there was no easy fix, forcing the character to make a tough choice between his old life and his new friend.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p><strong>Communication:</strong> The definition states that this means sharing ideas clearly in your writing. To make the story work, I had to use words that showed how the characters felt instead of just saying it directly. For example, when Brad leaves his family, I wrote that his dad looked down at the floor and held his mouth tight while he made a promise. Showing sadness through body language takes a lot of effort with your vocabulary.</p>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <div class="pullquote">

          <p>I use critical thinking outside of school when things go wrong in regular life, like when my bike breaks down or when my team is losing a sports game. Instead of getting mad, I have to stop, look at what is broken, and come up with a fast plan to fix the situation so we can keep going.</p>

        </div>

      </div>


      <div class="reveal">

        <p>My teacher told me I need to add more physical descriptions during the exciting parts of the story. I should describe Brad's breathing or his shaking hands so the reader can feel the tension. My next step is to plan my pacing better, because my endings usually happen way too fast.</p>

      </div>
